# Zimplifyed.ai — Marketing Plan to 1 Lakh Users

Owner: CMO. Executes VISION_1B §7 and GTM_PLAN.md, re-scoped from "paying orgs to $1M ARR" to the founder's expanded goal: **100,000 users (1 lakh), primarily Indian exporters/SMBs.** Metric vocabulary per specs/METRICS_DEFINITIONS.md; funnel mechanics per SELF_SERVE_PLAN.md; page/tool execution per specs/SEO_CONTENT_PLAN.md. GTM_PLAN.md remains the revenue plan; this document is the *user-growth* plan layered on it — the two reconcile in §9.

Working unit: **user = a human with an account** (User row, verified). Avg 2.4 users/org observed target (owner + ops + accounts in a 3-person firm; SELF_SERVE §9 invite-acceptance ≥60%). So **1 lakh users ≈ ~42,000 orgs.**

## 1. Positioning & messaging

### 1.1 Category: "The AI-first Export OS" — one umbrella
One sentence, everywhere:

> **Zimplifyed runs your entire export business — inquiry to money in the bank — with AI doing the paperwork, the follow-ups, and the filings prep.**

The umbrella expansion (unified inbox → CRM → AI quotes; scan-expense → AI-classify → GST; compliance; shipping) is marketed as **one story, not four products**: *"You don't need a CRM plus Tally plus a compliance consultant plus Excel. You need one system that already knows what an exporter does."* Modules are chapters of the same pitch.

### 1.2 Why we win the comparison (publish as /compare pages, M6)
| vs | Their frame | Our counter (persona-true) |
|---|---|---|
| **Zoho (CRM/Books/One)** | Horizontal suite, you configure it | Zoho doesn't know what a shipping bill, LUT, or e-BRC is. We ship export-native: HS codes, Incoterms, RoDTEP, doc cross-checks — day one, no consultant. AI does the work, not just the fields. |
| **TallyPrime** | The accountant's ledger | Tally records what happened; Zimplifyed runs what happens next — quotes, LC checks, doc-sets, payment realization. We sync *to* Tally (VISION_1B §6 backlog), we don't fight the CA. Message: "Keep Tally. Stop running your export business in it." |
| **Generic CRMs (HubSpot/Salesforce/Kylas)** | Pipeline software | An exporter's deal isn't closed at "won" — it's closed at e-BRC. We own inquiry→realization; a CRM owns 20% of it. Also: WhatsApp-first, INR/GST-native, per-outcome pricing vs seat taxes. |
| **IndiaMART/Alibaba** | Lead generation | Complementary, never competitive: "IndiaMART gives you the inquiry. Zimplifyed wins and ships the order." (GTM §3.3 stance — locked.) |
| **Excel + WhatsApp (the real incumbent)** | Free, familiar | We don't ask them to leave WhatsApp — we live in it (VISION_1B L-voice/WA-native). Free tier + Excel import everywhere makes switching a 20-minute event, not a project. |

### 1.3 Message hierarchy (persona-specific, per GTM §1 — unchanged, extended for the umbrella)
- **Founder/owner:** "Know your true margin on every order. Cash flow, incentives owed, P&L per order — one screen."
- **Merchant trader:** "Compare vendor rates, protect margin, quote in minutes — and every document AI-checked before the LC sees it."
- **Manufacturer-exporter:** "Production, QC, capacity, costing tracked against the order — not in three registers."
- **Ops manager:** "Nothing expires, nothing mismatches. LUT, e-BRC, cert renewals watched by AI."
- **First-time exporter (wedge):** "Got your first export inquiry? Guided step-by-step — IEC to realization."
- **New umbrella line (accounting):** "Photograph the expense, AI classifies it, GST-ready." — market as **"coming"** until shipped (positioning rule: roadmap ≠ here).

Claim hygiene: every public claim checked against shipped code before publish; per-doc accuracy numbers only once golden-eval metrics are stable (METRICS §5).

## 2. ICP segments & market sizing

India export universe (DGFT/ministry public figures, stated as planning assumptions):
- **IEC holders issued (cumulative):** ~8–9 lakh; **active exporters (≥1 shipment/yr):** ~1.4–1.6 lakh firms.
- **New IECs issued per year:** ~80,000–1,00,000 (the "first-time exporter" river — refills annually).
- **Exporting-capable SMBs / import-side firms / aspiring exporters on IndiaMART-Alibaba:** ~8–10 lakh firms.
- Avg 2–4 relevant seats per firm (founder, ops, accounts, sales).

| Segment | Firms (est.) | Users (est.) | Our role |
|---|---|---|---|
| S1 Bullseye: merchant + small manufacturer exporters, ₹2–50Cr, ≥2 shipments/mo | ~60,000 | ~180,000 | Paid core |
| S2 Active exporters, all sizes/verticals | ~1.5 lakh | ~4 lakh | Free→paid |
| S3 New IEC holders / first-timers per year | ~90,000/yr | ~1.2 lakh/yr | Free wedge, activation river |
| S4 CA firms & export consultants (multiplier) | ~15,000 practices | — | Channel: each brings 10–100 orgs |
| S5 Ecosystem: CHAs, forwarders, vendors on RFQs | ~50,000 | ~1 lakh | Free counterpart consoles → flywheel |

**Conclusion: 1 lakh users = ~10–12% of the reachable Indian user pool. Aggressive but not TAM-constrained.** No overseas dependency needed (buyer-side is upside, not required).

`signups(t) = pages_indexed × visits/page × 3% + tool_sessions × 10% + community_size × 4%/mo + WAU_orgs × viral_factor(0.25 invites accepted/org/mo) + partner_orgs × 2.4 + paid`
Compounding levers: pages (0→2,000 by end-2027), community (0→25k members by 2028), viral factor (branding + invite links), partner count (0→150 active by 2029).

## 5. Launch sequence

**Phase 0 — Waitlist (2026 Q3, weeks 1–8):** "Export OS" landing + 2 live tools (HS finder, landed-cost — they work without signup, per free-tool rule); waitlist gated on "which document wastes most of your week?" (segmentation data); 10 design-partner tenants free-for-feedback. Target: 2,000 waitlist, 1k tool sessions/wk.

**Phase 1 — Private beta (2026 Q4):** invite waitlist in tranches of 200; white-glove 30-min onboarding calls (research budget); ship RoDTEP calculator + doc-checklist generator; WhatsApp community #1 (textiles); circular-digest broadcast starts; first 200 programmatic pages. Target: **1,000 users**, activation ≥40%, 3 case studies drafted.

**Phase 2 — Public self-serve launch (2027 Q1):** open signup + paid plans (needs SELF_SERVE Phase C billing); LC checker tool (the PQL magnet); referral program; 800 pages; CA-partner pilot (5 firms); Hindi YouTube stream; Product Hunt-equivalent for India = FIEO/EPC newsletters + trade-press PR, not PH. Target: 4,000 users by Q1-end.

**Phase 3 — Doc-set (L2) moment (2027 Q2):** "AI does your shipment paperwork" campaign — biggest marketing beat of 2027; webinars with an EPC; vertical expansion (spices, handicrafts) Q3; State-of-Exports report Q4. Target: **10,000 users by end-2027.**

**Phase 4 — Scale machine (2028–29):** partner program GA (150 CA/consultant partners), vernacular content at full cadence, fair booths, agents (L3) launch campaign, buyer-side loops. Target: **35k users end-2028, 1 lakh mid/late-2029.**

Sensitivity: if organic→signup lands at 2% instead of 3%, 1 lakh slips ~2 quarters — the buffer is the partner channel (each active CA partner ≈ 25–60 users/yr) and the S3 first-timer river (90k new IECs/yr, owned via the readiness quiz + guided-first-order content).

## 8. Risks (delta vs GTM §10)
- **100k free users ≠ business**: guard the 8% free→paid and AI-COGS ≤20% of plan revenue (METRICS §3) — free-tier AI actions stay capped at 20/mo.
- **Vernacular content quality**: compliance claims in Hindi/Tamil reviewed by the domain-expert hire same as English — a wrong RoDTEP number in a viral Short is worse than none.
- **WhatsApp policy risk**: communities + broadcast comply with WABA rules; email list built in parallel from day one as the fallback.
